Ingredients

  • 1 cup semolina (rava)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1-2 green chilies (chopped)
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • 1/2 inch ginger (grated)
  • 1/4 cup carrots (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup peas
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp coriander leaves (chopped)

Steps

  1. Dry roast the semolina in a pan until fragrant. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard and cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
  3. Add chilies, onions, and ginger, saut√© until onions turn soft.
  4. Stir in the carrots and peas, cook for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Pour in water and bring to a boil.
  6. Slowly add semolina while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  7. Cook for 2-3 minutes until it thickens.
  8. Add lemon juice and garnish with coriander. Serve hot!

FAQs

Can I use coarse rava instead of fine rava?

Yes! Coarse rava gives a more textured upma, but fine rava makes it softer.

How do I make my upma non-sticky?

Dry roasting the rava properly prevents stickiness.
